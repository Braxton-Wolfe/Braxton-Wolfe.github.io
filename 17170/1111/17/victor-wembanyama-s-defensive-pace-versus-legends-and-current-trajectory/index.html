Victor Wembanyama's Defensive Pace Versus Legends And Current Trajectory

2025-11-17

Victor Wembanyama was regarded as the best prospect to enter the NBA since Lebron James over two decades ago. A primary reason for this was his elite two way ability, specifically his ability to impact the defensive side of the ball at a level we do not see in the NBA today. While a lot of that defensive hype he has gotten is because of his extraordinary size, there are plenty of other key factors and areas that Wembanyama thrives in to make his impact felt so much on the basketball court.

To begin looking into Wembanyama’s defensive impact I wanted to look at one question in two different ways. I decided to look into both simple box score statistics combined with advanced analytical statistics to answer the question: Is Victor Wembanyama on pace to become the greatest and most impactful defender the NBA has ever seen before.

In my first table chart, I wanted to compare Wembanyama to some of the greatest defenders of each generation. Specifically, comparing him to some of the best defending bigs the world has ever seen. For this table chart I wanted to look at some of the advanced metrics and compare Wembanyama’s first 127 career games to these greats to see how he is stacking up to those defensive legends and figure out the trajectory he is at in claiming the most impactful defender of all time spot. In this chart, the defensive statistics that I focused on were Defensive Win Shares, Block Percentage, Defensive Rebound Percentage, and Defensive Box Plus/Minus. Quick Check

Victor Wembanyama led in three out of the four categories that this table chart looked into, while Tim Duncan, was the only one to steal a single category over Wembanyama. Wembanyama’s block percentage really stood out from the others. In this instance, an estimated 10% of two point field goal attempts while Victor Wembanyama was in the game have been blocked, doubling everyone on this list except for Anthony Davis, who has been considered by many the best defender of the past decade.

On top of block percentage, Victor Wembanyama claimed the most defensive win shares as well as defensive rebounding percentage, and it was not particularly close. When it comes to some of these advanced defensive statistics, it is clear that Victor Wembanyama’s first 127 games are on pace to surpass some of the greatest defenders in NBA history through their first 127 games.

get_averages <- function(df, name) {
  df <- df |>
    filter(!str_detect(Season, "Yrs")) |>
    mutate(
      G = as.numeric(G),
      BLK = as.numeric(BLK),
      STL = as.numeric(STL),
      DRB = as.numeric(DRB),
      MP = as.numeric(MP)
    ) |>
    arrange(Season) |>
    mutate(cum_games = cumsum(G))
  
  df_127 <- df |> filter(cum_games <= 127 | lag(cum_games, default = 0) < 127)
  
  total_games <- sum(df_127$G)
  
  tibble(
    Player = name,
    Blocks = weighted.mean(df_127$BLK, df_127$G, na.rm = TRUE),
    Steals = weighted.mean(df_127$STL, df_127$G, na.rm = TRUE),
    Def_Rebounds = weighted.mean(df_127$DRB, df_127$G, na.rm = TRUE),
    Games = total_games
  )
}
